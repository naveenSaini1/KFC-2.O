Journey of the KFC Bucket

Our story begins with the man, the myth, the legend himself, Colonel Sanders. In 1930, Sanders bought a roadside hotel and café in Corbin, Kentucky where he used to serve hungry travelers with southern style chicken. He then spent years perfecting his now famous blend of 11 herbs and spices. Right until his demise in 1980, Colonel Sanders never stopped working for KFC. To this day, people are still vying to get their hands on the elusive recipe that has remained under wraps.

Today we are present with over 41,000 KFC restaurants in more than 125 countries and territories. And you know what? There’s still a cook in a kitchen in every last one of them, freshly preparing delicious, finger lickin’ good food!

KFC in India

1995

The very first KFC restaurant opened in Bangalore. Fast forward to the present, when there are over 450 restaurants across the country.

2008

In a first for the QSR industry in India, KFC launched the first Special KFC that employs speech and hearing-impaired team members.

2013

India’s first ever KFC restaurant to be managed and powered by an all-women was launched in Darjeeling

2021

The 25,000th KFC restaurant globally was launched in Hyderabad. Making it more special is the fact that this restaurant is powered by an all-women crew.
